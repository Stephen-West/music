Viol and recorder music

This site is a collection of free music, mostly from the renaissance and mostly transcribed into modern notation, by me, for viols or recorders. Some of these pieces are also available on the ISMLP website but, since the introduction of a membership plan on that site, I have decided to offer all my music for free download from my own site. The collection currently contains {{pieces}} pieces of music by {{composers}} composers.

The recorder music uses treble, octave treble or bass clef, as appropriate. The viol music is in string clefs, with tenor parts in alto clef.

File formats

All the music is downloadable as pdf files, which are generally formatted as A4 pages. In most cases, there are separate pdfs for parts and score. Mostly, I have typeset the music using the abc language and the excellent abcm2ps application for generating printable scores. I am gradually adding the abc source files and making them available for download. My usual method of working is to write out the abc notes in separate files and then use a script to automate the assembly of them into a single abc file, using the abc pre-processor, and the conversion to a final pdf file. The 'machine-assembled' abc files may not end up as well laid out as they would if the whole operation was done by hand.
